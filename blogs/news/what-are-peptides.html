    What are peptides?

    March 11, 2023
    Share

    Each of us has heard about proteins – the fundamental "building blocks" of our body. But what are peptides, and why do they hold such an important place in modern science and medicine? Let's dive into this topic!

    Introduction to the World of Peptides: Basic Concepts

    The world of biology and chemistry is vast and continuously expanding. One of the most interesting and actively researched areas is related to peptides. But what are they, and why are they so important?

    What are they?

    Peptides are short chains of amino acids linked together. Depending on the number of amino acids in the chain, peptides can be classified in different ways, let's see how:

    • Classification:

    Dipeptides: Contain two amino acids.

    Tripeptides: Contain three amino acids.

    Oligopeptides: Typically have between 4 and 20 amino acids.

    Polypeptides: Chains of many amino acids, which can reach protein sizes.

    • Biological Role of Peptides:

    Peptides have various functions in living organisms:

    Hormones: Many hormones in the body are peptides by origin. For example, insulin, which regulates blood sugar levels, is a peptide.

    Enzymes: Some of the enzymes involved in biochemical reactions in the body are peptides.

    Antimicrobial peptides: These peptides participate in the immune defense of the organism, helping to destroy harmful microorganisms.

    Neuropeptides: Serve to transfer information between neurons in the nervous system.

    Peptides are fundamental biomolecules with numerous functions in living organisms. They play a key role in many biological processes and have immense potential in the field of medicine and biotechnologies.

    Functions of the Peptides in the Body:

    Although they may be less known to the general public compared to molecules like DNA and proteins, they play a critical role in numerous biological processes. Let's examine some of the main functions in the human body.

    • Regulation of metabolic processes:

    Some of the hormones in our organism are peptides. An example of this is insulin, responsible for regulating glucose in the blood. Besides it, oxytocin is another peptide hormone, which plays an important role in the childbirth process.

    • Protection from microorganisms:

    Some peptides act as little soldiers, protecting our body from unwanted microorganisms. These antimicrobial peptides kill bacteria, fungi, and viruses by embedding in their membranes and destroying them.

    • Communication in the nervous system:

    Peptides can act as mediators of information between neurons. For example, endorphins are neuropeptides that are involved in regulating the sensation of pain and pleasure.

    • Support of tissue integration: Collagen and Elastin

    These peptides are essential for the structure of the skin and other connective tissues, providing strength and elasticity.

    • Acceleration of chemical reactions:

    Some enzymes, which assist the biochemical reactions in our cells, are peptides. They optimize the conversion of our food into energy.

    Peptides are vital molecules that support numerous processes in the human body. Although they often remain in the shadow of other biomolecules, they play no less significant a role in maintaining our health and well-being.

    How are peptides synthesized under laboratory conditions?

    Peptides, these small and complex molecules, play a vital role in biology and medicine. Although naturally occurring in living organisms, there is often a need to synthesize specific peptides in laboratory conditions. This process is both an art and a science. Let's look at the main steps of peptide synthesis in the laboratory.

    Determining the Method

    There are two main methods for peptide synthesis: solid-phase peptide synthesis (SPPS) and solution-phase synthesis. However, SPPS is widely used as it is more suitable for automated processes and for creating short to moderately long peptides.

    Solid-Phase Peptide Synthesis (SPPS)

    Activation: The first amino acid is attached to a resin, with its amino group being protected by special molecules.

    Sequential Bonding: Amino acids are added one by one. After each new amino acid is attached, the protective groups are removed to allow the next one to be attached.

    Release: Once the peptide chain is complete, the peptide is released from the resin, and the protective groups are removed.

    Finalization and Verification

    After synthesis is complete, the peptides often contain unwanted molecules or impurities. They need to be isolated and purified through methods like chromatography. Subsequently, the peptides are analyzed to confirm their structure and purity.

    Laboratory synthesis of peptides is a complex and precise process that requires specialized skills and equipment. However, thanks to innovations in technology and methodology, it is now possible to create peptides with high degrees of purity and specificity, which is crucial for scientific research and medical applications.

    Peptide Therapy: The Therapeutic Role of Peptides in Modern Medicine

    With the development of medical sciences, new and innovative treatment methods continue to penetrate clinical practice. Peptides, until recently known mostly in the field of cosmetics and biochemistry, now occupy an important place in the therapeutic strategies of medicine. Let's delve into the depths of their role in treating various diseases.

    The Therapeutic Role of Peptides in Modern Medicine

    Hormonal Regulation: Numerous peptides, such as insulin and glucagon, are crucial for regulating physiological functions in the body. They are used to treat diseases like type 1 and type 2 diabetes.

    Cardiovascular Diseases: Peptides can play a role in regulating blood pressure, targeting receptors involved in the contraction of blood vessels or affecting the balance of water and salt in the body.

    Immune Regulation: Some peptides have the potential to modulate the immune response, thereby helping in the fight against autoimmune diseases or boosting the immune system against infections.

    Oncology: In the field of oncology, they can be used as targeted therapeutic agents that attack specific molecular markers of tumor cells.

    Wound Healing: Peptides with antimicrobial activity can be used for the prevention and treatment of infections, especially in situations with resistant bacterial strains.

    • Advantages of Peptide Therapy

    One of the main advantages of peptides is their specificity. They can bind to specific receptors with high affinity, reducing the likelihood of side effects. Moreover, due to their small size and biodegradability, peptides often have a better safety profile compared to other drugs.

    Peptide therapy represents one of the most promising approaches in contemporary medicine. As our understanding of the molecular mechanisms of various diseases expands, peptides continue to take a more central place in the development of new, more effective therapies.

    Storage and Stability of Peptides: Practical Tips

    In the world of science, medicine, and cosmetics, peptides occupy a special place. They are short chains of amino acids that have various biological functions in the body. To ensure the best results from them, however, it is important to store them correctly. This article will present you with basic practical tips on the subject.

    • Storage Temperature

    In most cases, peptides are stored at low temperatures. Their stability is often greater when stored in a refrigerator or freezer. Before use, it is recommended to check the label for specific storage temperature instructions.

    • Protection from Light

    Some peptides can degrade or alter their properties when exposed to direct light. Therefore, it is important to store them in a dark place.

    • Minimize Contact with Moisture

    Many peptides are sensitive to moisture. It can disrupt their structure or functionality. Ensure the packaging is tightly closed and does not allow moisture to penetrate inside.

    Storing peptides requires special attention and caution. Following the principles mentioned above can guarantee optimal effectiveness and functionality. Be informed and always act in the interest of your scientific or cosmetic activity!
